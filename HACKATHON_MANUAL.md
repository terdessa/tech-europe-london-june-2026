To compete in the hackathon and have your project considered by the jury, all participants must adhere to the following rules and submission requirements. **Failure to meet these guidelines may result in disqualification.**

**Project Presentation**

- Record a **2-minute video demo** of your project (using Loom or equivalent platform)

  - Detailed explanation of your solution
  - Demonstration of key features with a live walkthrough

**Open Source Repository**

  - Clear documentation of all APIs, frameworks, and tools utilized

Our hackathon features a **two-stage competition format**, culminating in a **live final presentation** event.

- Build anything aligned with your creative vision — complete freedom of topic choice

- All finalists will showcase their projects **live before the jury and audience**

- These top 5 teams will be awarded the Finalist Stage Prizes

## Hackathon Tracks, Side Challenges & Prizes

### Tracks

#### Attio — The Agentic CRM

**🏆 Track Prize: iPad per team member**

**Challenge**

Most CRMs are glorified spreadsheets. You enter data, run queries, read lists. But that's not how sales, recruiting, or customer work actually gets done.

Attio is built on a different premise: a CRM shouldn't just hold context, it should act on it. That's what agentic CRM means in practice. Your customer data becomes the foundation for agents that work autonomously, around the clock, without anyone needing to log in.

**What we want you to build**

An agent or headless system that automates a meaningful chunk of a sales, recruiting, or customer workflow — the work people repeat every day: following up on leads, researching contacts, updating deal status from a call, drafting outreach.

The north star: **can your agent close a deal without even having a human in the loop?**

Hacky is fine, ambitious is better. If you see a problem worth solving that isn't on this list, build that instead.

**Some directions worth exploring**

- **Proactive Relationship Agent** — A scheduled agent that monitors pipeline staleness, decides who needs attention, drafts and sends a follow-up, then logs the outcome back to Attio. The hard part isn't the lookup, it's the autonomous loop.
- **Outbound Email Agent** — Identifies a target segment in Attio, enriches each record with external data, drafts personalised outreach, sends it, and writes results back. Fully headless, no human in the loop.
- **Inbound Lead Router** — Takes a new lead from a form, email, or webhook, enriches it from public sources, scores it, routes it to the right owner, and fires an initial response before anyone has looked at it.
- **Deal Risk Monitor** — Watches signals across emails, call transcripts, and deal metadata to detect deals going cold, then autonomously drafts an internal alert or suggested next step and pushes it to the right place.
- **Research-to-CRM Pipeline** — Pulls structured data on a company or person from public sources and populates a full Attio record ready for outreach. The enrichment pipeline is the challenge; writing back to Attio is the easy part.

**How to build on Attio** — four entry points:

- **Workflows** — Build custom workflow blocks with your own triggers and logic (just shipped this month)
- **Attio MCP** — Wire Attio into any MCP-compatible agent. Also supported inside custom agent blocks in Workflows
- **REST API** — Full programmatic access to the Attio data model
- **App SDK** — Build deeper integrations and custom surfaces inside Attio

Free Attio workspaces will be available on the day. Bring your own data or mock it, either works.

#### Minima (TUI or SDK)

**Minima** is Mubit's hosted model recommender service: describe a task, it recommends the cheapest LLM expected to clear your quality bar, you run that model in your own stack, then feed back the outcome so the next pick gets sharper. Use it to stop paying frontier prices for tasks a small model could handle — with no proxy, no prompt rewriting, and zero added latency to your real LLM call.

**Step 1 — Get a `MUBIT_API_KEY` (~1 minute)**

Minima authenticates with your Mubit key — same key, no extra Minima signup.

1. Sign in at console.mubit.ai and create a project (any name works).
2. In your project, click **Connect → Copy your API key** (`mbt_…`) — this is your `MUBIT_API_KEY`.
3. Open **Billing** and redeem the hackathon credit code: `MUBIT-LONDON-HACKATHON-JUNE` — generous **$2,000** credits for the full event.

**Step 2 — Pick your path** (TUI = fastest, no code; Python SDK = drop into your stack). Both hit the same hosted API.

**Path 1 — Minima TUI (Homebrew)**

1. Install: `brew install mubit-ai/minima/minima`
2. Run `minima` in any directory — the TUI opens
3. Type `/config` and paste your `MUBIT_API_KEY` plus at least one LLM provider key (OpenAI / Anthropic / Google / …)
4. Ask Minima to recommend a model, run it, then send feedback with the outcome + realized tokens

**Path 2 — Minima Python SDK**

1. Install: `pip install minima-cli`
2. Set your key: `export MUBIT_API_KEY=mbt_…`
3. First recommendation + feedback loop:

```python
from minima_client import MinimaClient

with MinimaClient("https://api.minima.sh", api_key="mbt_…") as minima:
    rec = minima.recommend(
        "Summarize this incident report into 3 bullets.",
        cost_quality_tradeoff=3,
    )
    print(rec.recommended_model.model_id)

    # run rec.recommended_model.model_id in your own stack, then:
    minima.feedback(
        rec.recommendation_id,
        rec.recommended_model.model_id,
        "success",
        quality_score=0.95,
        input_tokens=180,
        output_tokens=640,
        actual_cost_usd=0.0034,
    )
```

Async clients (`AsyncMinimaClient`), multi-step `recommend_workflow()`, `savings()` / `calibration()` reports, and zero-code `autocapture` are all in the SDK reference.
